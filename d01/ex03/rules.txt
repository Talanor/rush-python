In this exercise you will have to do a "bristromathique-like" program.

Your program will be named "bistromatique.py" and will receive his arguments through parameters.

example of use:

	./bistromatique.py "1+2+3"
	6
	./bistromatique.py "1+9/3"
	4
	./bistromatique.py "(1 + 2) + 3 - 145 * ((3 * 8) + 4 * (145 / 5)) + 8"
	-20286.0

All the input will be given in decimal, you don't have to handle all the basis
